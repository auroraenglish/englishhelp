Fundamentals
Parts of speech

Nouns:
 Nouns represent people, places, things, or ideas.
The cat is on the couch.
Verbs:
Verbs express actions, states, or occurrences.
He runs every morning.
Adjectives:
Adjectives describe or modify nouns.
It's a beautiful day.
Adverbs:
Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, providing more information about how, when, or where an action takes place.
She speaks English fluently." In this sentence, "fluently" is the adverb describing how she speaks.

Word order

Subject-Verb-Object Order:
English typically follows the subject-verb-object order in sentences
She (subject) plays (verb) the piano (object).

Adjective Order in Sentences:
Adjectives usually follow a specific order: opinion, size, age, shape, color, proper adjective, and purpose.
I bought a beautiful, small, old, round, red Italian table.

Questions

Yes/no questions expect a yes or no answer, while wh-questions seek more information.
Example: "Did you go to the store?" (Yes/No) "Where did you go?" (Wh-Question)

Tenses
Present simple

The present simple tense is used for actions that happen regularly , facts, or general truths.
Example: "I eat breakfast every morning."

Past simple

The past simple tense is used for completed actions in the past .
Example: "She visit ed Paris last summer."

Will Future

Usage: The "will" future is a common way to express future actions, predictions, promises, and spontaneous decisions.
Example: "I will call you later."
Note: This form is often used for actions that are not planned or decided beforehand.

Present Simple

Usage: The present simple tense is occasionally used to express future events in scheduled timetables, fixed arrangements, or facts.
Example: "The train leaves at 2 PM tomorrow."
Note: This usage is more common in formal contexts or with timetables.
